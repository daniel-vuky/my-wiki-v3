Notes on Distributed Systems

#systems #distributed Updated June 21, 2026

Working notes on building systems that stay correct when machines, networks, and time refuse to cooperate. Mostly distilled from incidents — and a few good papers.

Most "consensus" bugs in production aren't algorithmic — they're timeout and retry logic interacting badly under partial failure.

Consensus & replication

At its core, consensus is the problem of getting a distributed set of nodes to agree on a single value, even when some of them fail. The hard part isn't agreement — it's agreement under uncertainty about who is still alive.

# quorum: a strict majority must agree
def elect_leader(nodes):
    quorum = len(nodes) // 2 + 1
    votes  = gather_votes(nodes)
